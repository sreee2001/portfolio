Our Approach

Intelligent automation that respects your workflow.

SentinelBridge+ isn't just another email parser. It's a purpose-built classification engine designed for IT operations teams who demand precision, transparency, and control.

The Problem We Solve

Every IT helpdesk receives hundreds (or thousands) of support emails daily. Sorting these emails manually is slow, error-prone, and drains analyst time. Traditional keyword-based rules are brittle and require constant maintenance. Emails get mis-classified, duplicates slip through, and SLA clocks start ticking before anyone even sees the request.

SentinelBridge+ eliminates this bottleneck by applying machine learning to classify emails with human-level accuracy, automatically create ServiceNow tickets, and notify the right teams in real time.

How Classification Works

Step 1: Email Ingestion

SentinelBridge+ monitors your support mailbox (e.g., support@yourcompany.com) in near real-time via the Microsoft Graph API. When a new email arrives, it's queued for processing. Thread metadata is extracted to enable correlation with existing tickets.

Step 2: AI-Powered Analysis

The email body, subject, sender, and any attachments are analyzed by our fine-tuned GPT-4 model. The model outputs:

  • Intent: Incident, Service Request, or Question
  • Urgency: Critical, High, Medium, or Low
  • Category: Hardware, Software, Network, Access, etc.
  • Assignment Group: Based on historical patterns and your org structure
  • Confidence Score: 0–100% indicating model certainty

Step 3: Thread Correlation

Before creating a new ticket, SentinelBridge+ checks for related emails using message IDs (In-Reply-To, References headers) and semantic similarity. If a match is found, the email is appended to the existing ticket as a note or comment.

Step 4: Ticket Creation or Review

If the confidence score is above your threshold (default: 85%), a ServiceNow ticket is created automatically. For lower-confidence classifications, an adaptive card is sent to Teams for analyst review. Analysts can approve, edit, or reject the suggested classification.

Step 5: Continuous Learning

Every time an analyst corrects a classification, that feedback is logged and used to retrain the model. Over time, accuracy improves and adapts to your organization's unique language, acronyms, and ticket patterns.
