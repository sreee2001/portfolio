The Incident Lifecycle

Step 1: Declare the Incident

When something breaks, type /incident in any Teams channel or click the Incident Commander bot message extension. The bot prompts you for:

  • Severity (P0 = critical outage, P1 = major, P2 = minor)
  • Short description
  • Affected service or system

Step 2: Auto-Create War Room

The bot instantly creates a dedicated Teams channel named "INC-1234" and @mentions the incident commander, technical lead, and responders you specify. Stakeholders receive an automatic notification in your leadership channel.

Step 3: Follow the Runbook

Pre-loaded checklists appear as adaptive cards in the war room. For example, a "Database Outage" runbook includes:

  • Check DB cluster health
  • Review recent deployments
  • Fail over to backup instance
  • Notify customers via status page

Team members check off tasks as they complete them.

Step 4: Post Updates

Use /update to broadcast status to stakeholders without leaving the war room. The bot cross-posts to your leadership channel and optionally updates your status page or ServiceNow ticket.

Step 5: Resolve & Export

When the incident is resolved, type /resolve. The bot archives the war room, exports a full timeline (who did what, when), and prompts you to schedule a postmortem meeting.

Quick Command Reference

/incident

Start a New Incident

Opens a dialog to declare an incident and create a war room channel.

/update [message]

Post Status Update

Broadcasts an update to stakeholders and logs it in the timeline.

/escalate

Escalate Severity

Bumps the incident up a severity level and notifies additional stakeholders.

/assign @user [role]

Assign Role

Designates someone as incident commander, tech lead, or communications lead.

/resolve

Mark Resolved

Closes the incident, archives the war room, and exports the timeline.

/runbook [name]

Load Runbook

Displays a checklist for a specific incident type (e.g., "database-outage").
