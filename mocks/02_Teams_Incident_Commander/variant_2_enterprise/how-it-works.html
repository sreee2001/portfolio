Incident Lifecycle Automation

1

Detection & Declaration

Incidents can be declared via:

  • Manual command in Teams (/incident)
  • Automated alert from monitoring tools (Datadog, PagerDuty, Splunk)
  • ServiceNow ticket creation trigger

The system captures severity (P0–P4), affected services, and initial context.

2

War Room Provisioning

A dedicated Teams channel is created with:

  • Auto-generated incident ID (INC-2025-0142)
  • Pre-configured tabs (Timeline, Runbook, Integrations, Metrics)
  • Role assignments (Commander, Tech Lead, Comms Lead) based on on-call schedule
  • Stakeholder notification to executive channel and configured distribution lists
3

Coordination & Execution

Team members execute the incident response using:

  • Interactive runbook checklists (approval required for critical steps)
  • Bot commands for status updates, escalation, and role reassignment
  • Real-time timeline capturing all actions, decisions, and communications
  • Integrated bridge lines (Teams/Zoom) for live coordination
4

Stakeholder Communication

Automated and manual update workflows:

  • Broadcast updates to multiple channels (Teams, Slack, email, SMS)
  • Optional customer-facing status page integration
  • Escalation notifications when SLA thresholds approach
  • Executive summary cards with key metrics (MTTR, affected users)
5

Resolution & Postmortem

Close the incident and generate learning artifacts:

  • Mark resolved with required resolution notes
  • Auto-generate postmortem template with timeline, root cause, and action items
  • Export to Confluence, Notion, or internal wiki
  • Schedule postmortem meeting with all participants
  • Track action items as Jira tickets with automatic follow-up
